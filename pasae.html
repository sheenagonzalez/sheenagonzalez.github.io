PASAE

From PR Coordinator to Creative Director, I lead the creative team in generating ideas for and hands-on involvement with graphic design, marketing, photography and videography.

Motivation

College is an exciting, challenging and intimidating experience for most. PASAE’s mission is to build a tight-knit community of career-oriented students in technical fields. To flourish as one of eight Filipino student organizations targeted towards minorities in a large university, media management and marketing is a core necessity. As an effort to recruit more members and keep the community engaged in events, I ensured both offline and online presence on campus by managing media content including but not limited to posters, merch, photos and videos.

Posters

My main job in PR is advertising. For each event, I designed digital posters using Illustrator and posted them on Facebook so people could share and invite others. These events range from fundraisers and intern socials, to alumni dinners and professional workshops.

Shirts

Having cool clubwear not only serves as bragging rights, but also creates a sense of unity within the group. For this reason, I custom-designed apparels that are unique from year to year. While interns and general members have casual wear (on the left) that is easy to move around in, staff members also have their own personalized jackets (on the right) so that they can formally and dutifully represent PASAE in a professional manner when necessary.

Photography

As the head of the Creative Component, I learned to be flexible in undertaking different jobs when needed. This includes filling in for our one photographer in covering events such as member socials and company tours. To see the full albums, click each photo to redirect to PASAE's Facebook (link) page.

Making videos for PASAE is my first dive into recording and videoediting.
